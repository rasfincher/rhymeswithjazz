Simple Is Better

March 2, 2021

This is the first post on my newest blog. I tend to create a new blog every couple of years and go through a cycle of being extremely excited about it, then only occasionaly posting, and then dormancy. I make no claims that this time will be any different. What is different this time is that I'm not using any sort of blogging engine to create this blog. No Wordpress, no Drupal, no Jekyll, no Hugo. Just plain old HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Some would say "as God intended". I don't know about that. What I do know is that this will give me a chance to evaluate the things I need and the things I want. When I run across a problem that was easily solved with a blogging engine I'm going to have to either write my own solution, or decide I didn't really need it that much anyway. It's an experiment!

I was inspired by Rediscovering the Small Web, an essay celebrating the good old days of the internet. I'll be turning 41 in a few months, and I'm going through a reminiscent phase. I'm not going full "get off my lawn", but I do miss the days where it was simple to follow exactly what was going on on the web. Don't get me wrong, a ton of my day-to-day work is with frameworks like Angular and ASP.net, but there is something satisfying about the speed and simplicity of a website written in boring technology.

I look forward to updating this site with my experiences, good and bad. And maybe I'll even stick with this for longer than a month or two!
